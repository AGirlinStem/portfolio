On my journey with Electrical Engineering.

Elevate Internship


During my internship with Elevate, I worked on a variety of 3D printing projects, including resin sampling, creating remote holders, business cards, business card holders, and Voronoi vases. I also created lab samples for a sessional instructor and contributed to material testing by collecting tensile and torsion data for 3D printed materials. My testing focused on evaluating the mechanical properties of printed samples, including factors such as build orientation, slice settings, and the effect of various angles on material strength.

As part of my work, I conducted torsional testing and angle testing, deliberately testing failure points to analyze how different build angles impacted the material's performance and durability. In addition to these tasks, I printed various shapes in minimal sizes to test the accuracy and capabilities of the BAMBU Lab X1 Carbon printer. This hands-on work allowed me to assess the printer's precision and performance with complex geometries and small-scale prints.

This internship provided comprehensive exposure to both the practical and analytical aspects of 3D printing, solidifying my understanding of how different printing methods, settings, and angles affect material strength, accuracy, and overall performance.
