Rail Car


Team 46 designed a railcar to provide a humane and efficient method for transporting large animals by rail. The railcar incorporates a pivoting wheel system for enhanced maneuverability, allowing it to navigate tight spaces and make sharp turns. It includes windows for ventilation and food and water trays to ensure the animals' comfort during travel. The sliding door and low-grade ramp provide safe and easy loading and unloading, minimizing stress on the animals' joints.

The railcar is integrated with an Arduino-based robot, enabling autonomous navigation with obstacle avoidance and line tracking. The automatic coupling system ensures smooth attachment and detachment from the robot without manual intervention. The final prototype successfully met all requirements, transporting more animals than initially specified while navigating a preset course with minimal issues.

Safety features include reinforced side walls that act as a crumple zone to protect the animals in case of a collision. Despite limitations such as financial constraints and the inability to modify the robot, the design effectively achieved its goals, providing a reliable and scalable solution for animal transportation.
